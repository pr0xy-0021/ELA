Text Study

Romeo and Juliet

William Shakespeare

Two teenagers belonging to competing families fall in love and meet a dreadful end. Shakespeare's poetic style of writing and the subjects of love, hatred, and fate continue to echo with great relevance today.

The Absolutely True Diary of a Part-Time Indian

Sherman Alexie

Junior leaves his reservation school to find a better future, facing racism and identity struggles. The mix of humor and pain makes this book honest, relatable, and eye-opening.

The Book Thief

Markus Zusak

Set in Nazi Germany, The Book Thief follows Liesel, a girl who finds hope in stolen books during war. The narrator, Death, and the focus on language make this a unique and emotional read.

Finding My Voice After Years of Silence

This text explains how I fled Iran at age four because of religious persecution, then lived in fear and silence in Turkey. After moving to Canada, I began unlearning that silence. Now, I’m learning to speak openly and live without hiding who I am.

The Impact of Social Pressure in Romeo and Juliet

This text explains how social pressure influences the characters’ decisions in Romeo and Juliet. Romeo’s actions after Mercutio’s death and Juliet’s agreement to marry Paris show how family and societal expectations lead them to make tragic, impulsive choices they may not have made otherwise."

Reflection Letter

Dear Reader,

As a learner, writer, and reader, I like to think of myself as being someone who places a huge emphasis on stories and the feelings that they evoke within individuals. I love reading novels about difficult human relationships or ones that push boundaries in society—George Orwell, Khaled Hosseini, and Suzanne Collins are personal favorites of mine. I also like reading historical fiction and dystopian series since they allow me to imagine different worlds while still focusing on real concerns. Other than reading, my next favorite activity is viewing contemplative movies and documentaries, especially those with strong character development or compelling visual work. I listen to music with excellent lyrics—hip-hop, alternative, and indie are my most preferred. As a writer, I prefer writing in personal narrative, fiction, or oral modes, where I can express freely. I like writing work that is emotionally true or imaginatively structured, even if it is not presented in standard academic forms.

On English Language Arts, I generally excel at questions that involve creative writing, visual or multimedia, and anything where I get to think as myself or make connections between texts and real life. I sometimes struggle with literary analysis essays, especially when I am asked to deconstruct tricky symbolism or connect a few texts to a single theme. I learn best through examples, diagrams, and reviewing my work using feedback. I am also effective when I get to work at my own pace, especially when I have a clear checklist or rubric to work from. My writing procedure is usually to begin with brainstorming and writing down raw thoughts, followed by scribbling a rough draft in a hurry and then revising later. Reading my stuff aloud during revising is helpful for me to get a sense of what works and what doesn't. One thing I need to continue improving on is organization—I have a tendency to jump around too much in my thoughts without having good transitions. Overall, I consider myself an avid learner always looking for ways to grow as a thinker and communicator.

Sincerely,

Ariya
